**"AI is overhyped for dev work."**
I've heard this a lot.
But here's what people miss:

AI isn't here to _replace_ developers.
It's here to _supercharge_ the ones who learn how to wield it properly.

## The Real Shift

The shift won't be:
`100 devs → 0 devs`
It will be:
`100 devs → 10 elite devs + AI integrated into the workflow`

## Skills of the Future-Ready Developer

Here's what the future-ready developer looks like:

### Prompt Mastery

You don't just ask AI for code; you _design_ prompts that turn vague problems into perfect solutions.

### Context Injection

You know how to give AI the exact codebase slice, docs, or test cases it needs so it nails the answer first try.

### Dev Environment Integration

AI doesn't live in a chat tab; it works directly inside your editor, repo, and CI/CD pipeline.

### Live Knowledge Access

AI can instantly scan docs, APIs, and changelogs in real time—no more outdated "knowledge cutoffs."

### Stack-Aware AI Configs

You fine-tune AI with your framework's conventions, so it codes like someone who's been on your team for years.

## The Bottom Line

The next big skill gap isn't "Can you code?"
It's "Can you orchestrate an AI-assisted dev workflow that outproduces an entire team?"

The developers who master this will be the ones who thrive in the AI-augmented future.
